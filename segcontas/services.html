Strategic Consultancy

In recent years strategic planning has evolved and is now supported by very specific approaches. There is a group of very specific features for defining a strategy, and for the processes of putting it into practice.

At SEGCONTAS we help companies deploy successful strategies, monitoring each stage of the strategic planning. We not only help you define your strategy, but continue to guide you over the entire deployment process.

We support and develop:

  • The strategic planning cycle.
  • Preparation of the business plan.
  • Preparation of the marketing plan.
  • Studies of market trends.
  • Growth opportunities.

Financial Consultancy

Based on solid knowledge of economics and finance, corporate and risk management and strategic-financial planning, we help you to identify potential problems and to implement practical solutions that will help effectively improve your company’s financial management and performance, through economic and financial feasibility studies of your investments, and also evaluation of the most appropriate funding sources for your business.

We support and develop:

  • Preparation of the financial plan.
  • Budgeting and control process.
  • Decision on KPIs and implementation of scorecards.
  • Investment analysis.
  • Solutions for raising investment.

Organizational Model

SEGCONTAS assigns decision-making powers and duties to various different areas of the organizational structure, defining management levels. SEGCONTAS' structure is divided into four different levels, shown below.

SEGCONTAS – structure:

  • The Shareholders' General Meeting
  • The Board of Directors
  • The Audit Board
  • Management units
  • Departments

Main duties:

  • Shareholders' General Meeting
    • Supervise management of business and provides advice.
    • Manage appointments, compensation, and evaluation of top-level management.
    • Work to provide results for the interests of the shareholders.
  • Board of Directors and Audit Board
    • Decide internal policies and carry out planning and control of the business.
    • Manage corporate funds and service the shareholder or partner.
  • Management Units
    • Control of activity and the business performance.
  • Departments
    • Operational activities as per allocation, reporting to Management.
